Prepare Your Household

The best time to prepare is before a disaster. Start with these three steps — then go deeper.

1

Build Your 72-Hour Kit

In the first 72 hours after a major disaster, emergency services are overwhelmed. Your kit — food, water, medications, documents — keeps your family self-sufficient until help arrives.

Start Your Checklist →
2

Know Your Neighborhood

Find your Neighborhood Emergency Coordinator — your trained neighbor who is your first point of contact after a disaster. Know your evacuation zone before a wildfire warning is issued.

Find My Coordinator →
3

Make a Family Plan

Agree on a meeting point outside your home and one outside your neighborhood. Practice Drop-Cover-Hold On. Know how to shut off your gas. Have an out-of-area contact everyone can reach.

Hazard-Specific Guides

🔥

Wildfire

  • Know your evacuation zone (A–D)
  • Create defensible space around your home
  • Sign up for SD Emergency Alerts
  • Have a go-bag packed and ready
  • Plan 2 evacuation routes from your neighborhood

Resources: Ready San Diego · SDG&E Wildfire Safety

🌍

Earthquake

  • Drop, Cover, Hold On — practice it now
  • Strap water heaters and tall furniture
  • Know how to shut off your gas
  • Keep shoes and a flashlight under your bed
  • Be prepared for aftershocks

Resources: Earthquake Country Alliance · ShakeOut

🌊

Flood & Severe Rain

  • Turn Around, Don't Drown — never drive flooded roads
  • Clear gutters before rain season (Oct–Apr)
  • Know your flood zone (FEMA maps)
  • Free sandbags available at Poway Public Works
  • Move valuables off ground floors before storms

Resources: FEMA Flood Map · City of Poway

🌡️

Extreme Heat

  • Check on elderly and isolated neighbors daily
  • Never leave children or pets in parked cars
  • Know where cooling centers are in Poway
  • Stay hydrated — don't wait until you're thirsty
  • Call 2-1-1 during heat emergencies

Resources: 2-1-1 San Diego · City of Poway
