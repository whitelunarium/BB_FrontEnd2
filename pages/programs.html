What We Offer

Programs & Services

PNEC runs year-round programs to make every Poway neighborhood more resilient. All programs are free for Poway residents.

Core Programs

From hands-on training to neighborhood-level coordination, PNEC has a program for everyone.

🎓

CERT Training

Community Emergency Response Team

Poway's CERT program prepares ordinary residents to safely assist in disasters before professional responders arrive. The course covers first aid, light search and rescue, fire suppression, and disaster psychology.

  • 24 hours of instruction over three weekends
  • Free for all Poway residents
  • Offered twice per year (spring and fall)
  • Taught by City of Poway Fire Department staff

PACT Ham Radio

Poway Amateur Communications Team

When cell and internet infrastructure fails, PACT operators keep emergency communications flowing. PACT volunteers hold amateur radio licenses and are trained to relay critical information during disasters.

  • Operates on designated Poway emergency frequencies
  • Coordinates with San Diego OES and ARES/RACES
  • Licensing support provided through club study sessions
  • Regular training nets and drills throughout the year

Neighborhood Coordinators

Hyperlocal Emergency Network

PNEC's backbone is its network of 60+ trained Neighborhood Emergency Coordinators — residents who serve as the emergency point of contact for their street or block and who know their neighbors by name.

  • Covers every Poway residential neighborhood
  • Coordinators conduct annual household surveys
  • Maintain block-level evacuation plans
  • Connect neighbors with CERT and PACT resources

72-Hour Kit Workshops

Community Workshop

These hands-on sessions walk participants through building a complete 72-hour emergency go-bag. Facilitators review FEMA and local San Diego County guidelines and help attendees customize kits for their household needs.

  • Held at Poway Community Park and local libraries
  • Special sessions for seniors and families with children
  • Discounted bulk supply orders coordinated through PNEC
  • Use our online interactive checklist to prep in advance

Regional Partners

PNEC works in close coordination with county and state emergency management agencies.

Get Ready San Diego

PNEC is a registered partner of the San Diego County Office of Emergency Services' Get Ready San Diego campaign. Our programs align with county-wide preparedness standards and we share resources, training materials, and alerts with the OES network.

Visit Get Ready SD

San Diego County OES

The San Diego County Office of Emergency Services provides PNEC with grant funding, professional training, and coordination support. PNEC coordinators and CERT graduates are integrated into the county's Volunteer Organizations Active in Disaster (VOAD) framework.
