Get Involved with PNEC

Whether you can give two hours a year or become a frontline responder, there's a meaningful role for you in Poway's emergency preparedness network.

Volunteer Roles

All roles are open to Poway residents. No prior experience required — PNEC provides all training.

🏘️

Neighborhood Coordinator

Be the emergency point of contact for your street or block. Coordinators conduct annual household surveys, maintain a neighborhood resource inventory, and serve as the link between residents and PNEC leadership during a disaster.

  • Commitment: ~4 hours/month
  • Annual all-coordinator training included
  • Best for: community-minded residents who know their neighbors
🎓

CERT Volunteer

Complete PNEC's 24-hour CERT training and join a team that activates during local emergencies to support fire, medical, and search-and-rescue operations until professional responders arrive.

  • Commitment: 24-hr training + quarterly drills
  • Training provided free by the City of Poway Fire Dept
  • Best for: hands-on helpers ready to respond when called
📻

Ham Radio Operator

Get your FCC Technician license and join PACT — the Poway Amateur Communications Team. PACT operators provide emergency voice and data communications when cell and internet infrastructure fails.

  • Commitment: monthly nets + emergency activations
  • PNEC sponsors licensing exam study sessions
  • Best for: tech-savvy residents comfortable with radio equipment
📋

Administrative & Board Support

Help PNEC run its outreach campaigns, events, social media, grant writing, and organizational operations. Board members serve one-year terms and guide PNEC's strategic direction.

  • Commitment: varies by role (2–8 hrs/month)
  • Skills needed: writing, design, event planning, finance, IT
  • Best for: organized, community-focused residents
